  • Outline:
    • Review: random variables, Gaussian random vectors, Shannon sampling theorem.
    • Basic methods for stochastic simulation: inverse cumulative distribution function (cdf) method, von Neumann's rejection sampling, importance sampling etc.
    • Continuous-time random signals
      • Wide-sense stationary (WSS) signals and power spectral density, white noise, bandlimited random signals;
      • Applications: filtering WSS signals, noise analysis in circuits.
    • Sampling theorem for random signals, basic discrete-time random signals.
    • Optimal filtering from a statistical viewpoint, minimum mean-square error (MMSE) estimation, linear MMSE estimation, maximum a posteriori (MAP) estimation.
    • Basic detection: MAP rule, minimum probability of error, Chernoff bound on error probability, distributed detection.
    • Modern applications.
